Precision Machining
Michigan Machine Shop — Filling Open Capacity
A Michigan machine shop lost a major customer. Their newest screw machine was 90% open capacity. Sales relied on referrals and one inside rep routing everything to the GM. There was no structured outbound activity.
Goals
  • Fill open capacity quickly
  • Generate predictable RFQs
  • Work with fewer customers that offered more volume
What changed

Market repositioned away from automotive, medical, and aerospace toward electronics, agriculture, energy, and consumer goods — targeting companies above $50M revenue. Phone-first outreach: 113 calls/day, ~2,000/month with dedicated inside salesperson and sales manager oversight.

Goals

  • Generate qualified RFQs consistently
What was implemented

Targeted manufacturers in agriculture, mining, energy, construction, Tier 2 automotive, and machinery — companies above $100M revenue. Phone-first outreach: 111 calls/day, ~2,000/month with dedicated inside salesperson and weekly CRM/QA tracking.

Injection Molding
Global Injection Molder — Accessing Tier 1 Decision Makers
A global IATF-certified injection molder with a plant in central Mexico had capacity and capability but lacked consistent access to engineering and purchasing at Tier 1 suppliers. Previous sales reps had limited traction.
Goals
  • Fill open capacity with transfer business
  • Generate qualified RFQs consistently
  • Build inside sales and account management support
What was implemented

Identified Tier 1 suppliers with Mexican plants buying within required tonnage range. Mapped accounts across engineering, purchasing, quality, and corporate contacts. Phone-first outreach: 95 calls/day, ~2,000/month. Messaging centered on IATF accreditation, global sites, and equipment fit.

Is this a fit?
We're selective about who we work with.

This model works best for industrial companies that are ready to invest in a real sales program, not companies looking for cheap leads.

Good fit
Industrial companies doing $1M+ in revenue
You have a proven product and existing customers, but your sales pipeline depends on referrals, trade shows, or the owner's personal network. You need consistent outbound but don't want to hire, train, and manage a salesperson from scratch.
Good fit
Industrial companies selling into enterprise / F500 accounts
Long sales cycles, multiple decision-makers, technical products that require reps who actually understand the industrial space. You need someone who can have credible conversations with engineers and procurement teams.
Not a fit
Companies looking for a lead list or appointment setting
If you want a CSV of contacts or someone to book cold calls on your calendar, there are cheaper options. We run the full sales cycle, from outreach through close, and that's a different level of commitment.
Not a fit
Early-stage companies without product-market fit
If you're still figuring out what you sell or who you sell it to, an embedded salesperson won't fix that. We work best with companies that know their product works and need help scaling the sales motion.
